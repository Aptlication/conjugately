## What Comet Provides For Voice

- Comet has **“industry‑leading voice technology”** for interacting with the Comet Assistant and browsing via voice, including on mobile.[4][1]
- Recent Comet updates add **voice control of the browser** and cross‑tab voice interactions with the assistant, but these do not replace `window.speechSynthesis` inside arbitrary sites.[2][3]

## How This Interacts With Your TTS

- Your quiz’s TTS (`tts.speakQuestion()`, `tts.speakAnswer()`, `tts.stop()`) uses the browser’s Web Speech API, so compatibility hinges on whether the Comet browser exposes `speechSynthesis` to page scripts and allows audio autoplay after user interaction.[5]
- The diagnostic note about Replit’s embedded iframe having limited audio permissions remains relevant: Comet can improve general browsing and add its own voice features, but it does not automatically bypass iframe autoplay/permission constraints in the underlying engine.[5][2]
