The French **conditional** (le conditionnel) is used to express what **would** happen under certain conditions, similar to the English use of “would” plus a verb[2][3][5]. It is commonly used for polite requests, hypothetical situations, wishes, and reported speech.

**Key uses:**
- To state hypothetical or possible actions:  
  *Je voyagerais si j'avais de l'argent.*  
  (*I would travel if I had money.*)[1][2][6]
- To make polite requests or suggestions:  
  *Pourriez-vous m'aider ?*  
  (*Could you help me?*)[1][2][5]
- To express wishes or desires:  
  *Je voudrais un café.*  
  (*I would like a coffee.*)[2][6]
- In reported speech for future-in-the-past:  
  *Il a dit qu’il viendrait.*  
  (*He said he would come.*)[2][5]

**How to form it:**
- Use the **future tense stem** (usually the infinitive for regular verbs)
- Add the **imparfait endings**: **-ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient**[3][5]

| Pronoun  | Example verb: jouer ("to play") | Conditional Form | English          |
|----------|---------------------------------|------------------|------------------|
| je       | jouer                           | je jouerais      | I would play     |
| tu       | jouer                           | tu jouerais      | you would play   |
| il/elle  | jouer                           | il jouerait      | he/she would play|
| nous     | jouer                           | nous jouerions   | we would play    |
| vous     | jouer                           | vous joueriez    | you would play   |
| ils/elles| jouer                           | ils joueraient   | they would play  |

**In summary:**  
The French conditional is used much like the English “would,” and forms the backbone of discussing hypotheticals, politeness, and indirect speech in French[1][2][6].
