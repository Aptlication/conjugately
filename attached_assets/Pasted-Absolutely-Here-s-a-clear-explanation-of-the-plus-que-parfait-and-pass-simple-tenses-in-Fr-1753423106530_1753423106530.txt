## 1. **Plus-que-parfait**

### **What is it?**
- The **plus-que-parfait** is like the English past perfect (“had done”).  
- It’s used to talk about something that had happened before another action in the past.

### **How to form it?**
- **Imparfait** of **avoir** or **être** + past participle  
  (e.g., j’avais mangé – I had eaten)

### **Examples:**
- **French:** J’avais déjà mangé quand il est arrivé.  
  **English:** I had already eaten when he arrived.

- **French:** Elle était partie avant midi.  
  **English:** She had left before noon.

### **Summary:**
| French            | Example                 | English Equivalent       |
|-------------------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| Plus-que-parfait  | J’avais fini mon travail.| I had finished my work. |

## 2. **Passé Simple**

### **What is it?**
- The **passé simple** is a literary or formal past tense in French.
- It’s used for completed actions in the past and mostly found in novels, fairy tales, or formal writing—rarely in conversation.
- Similar meaning to **passé composé** (I did, I went, etc.), but never spoken.

### **How to form it?**
- Irregular and regular patterns, usually with endings like -a, -èrent, -it, -irent, etc.
    - **Il parla** (He spoke), **Ils finirent** (They finished), **Elle vit** (She saw)

### **Examples:**
- **French:** Il entra, regarda autour de lui, et s’assit.  
  **English:** He entered, looked around, and sat down.

- **French:** Elle écrivit une lettre.  
  **English:** She wrote a letter.

### **Summary:**
| French         | Example              | English Equivalent             |
|----------------|---------------------|--------------------------------|
| Passé simple   | Il parla             | He spoke                       |
| Passé simple   | Elle alla à Paris.   | She went to Paris.             |

## **Direct English Comparison Table:**

| French             | When to use                                    | English equivalent                       |
|--------------------|------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Plus-que-parfait   | Action before another past event                | Past perfect: “had done”                 |
| Passé simple       | Completed past actions, literary/written only   | Simple past: “did/went” (in stories)     |

**Note:**  
- **Plus-que-parfait** is used in spoken and written French, like English past perfect.
- **Passé simple** is almost only in books; in speaking, use **passé composé** instead.
