Here’s a compact **report on s’ennuyer** and a recap of the exercise answers that were checked.

## Core verb facts

- The verb is **s’ennuyer**, a reflexive \(-yer\) verb meaning “to be bored” or “to get bored.” [frenchcircles](https://www.frenchcircles.ca/sennuyer-present/)
- Present tense forms (indicative):  
  - je **m’ennuie**  
  - tu **t’ennuies**  
  - il/elle/on **s’ennuie**  
  - nous **nous ennuyons**  
  - vous **vous ennuyez**  
  - ils/elles **s’ennuient** [leconjugueur.lefigaro](https://leconjugueur.lefigaro.fr/conjugaison/verbe/ennuyer_pronominal.html)
- As a reflexive verb, it always takes a **reflexive pronoun** that matches the subject: me, te, se, nous, vous, se. [lingoculture](https://lingoculture.com/blog/grammar/french-reflexive-pronouns/)

## Negation and questions

- Negation wraps the verb and pronoun: **ne + reflexive + verb + pas** → *Je ne m’ennuie pas*, *Ils ne s’ennuient pas*. [larousse](https://www.larousse.fr/conjugaison/francais/s'ennuyer/4106)
- Questions can be formed in three main ways (all seen in the quiz):  
  - Statement with rising intonation: *Tu t’ennuies ?*  
  - **Est-ce que**: *Est-ce que je m’ennuie ?*  
  - Inversion (more formal): *Ne t’ennuies-tu pas ?*, *Ne s’ennuie-t-il pas ?* [preply](https://preply.com/en/blog/french-question-words/)
